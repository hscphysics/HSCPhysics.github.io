1.1 Motion in a Straight Line

Scalar vs Vector Quantities

Scalars have magnitude only: distance, speed, time, mass, energy.

Vectors have magnitude AND direction: displacement, velocity, acceleration, force.

üí° Remember
Distance is total path (scalar). Displacement is straight-line change with direction (vector).
Key Definitions
  • Average velocity: total displacement divided by time
  • Instantaneous velocity: velocity at a specific moment (gradient of x-t graph)
  • Acceleration: rate of change of velocity
$$v_{avg} = \frac{\Delta x}{\Delta t} = \frac{x_f - x_i}{t_f - t_i}$$ $$a_{avg} = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t}$$
üìù Example
A car travels 100 km north in 1 hour, then 50 km south in 30 minutes.

Total distance: 150 km
Displacement: 50 km north
Average speed: 150/1.5 = 100 km/h
Average velocity: 50/1.5 = 33.3 km/h north
Graphs of Motion

Position-time graphs: Gradient = velocity

Velocity-time graphs: Gradient = acceleration, Area = displacement

1.2 SUVAT Equations

For constant acceleration, we have five equations connecting displacement (s), initial velocity (u), final velocity (v), acceleration (a), and time (t).

$$v = u + at$$ $$s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$$ $$v^2 = u^2 + 2as$$ $$s = \frac{1}{2}(u+v)t$$
üí° Choosing Equations
  • No s? Use v = u + at
  • No v? Use s = ut + ¬Ωat¬≤
  • No t? Use v¬≤ = u¬≤ + 2as
  • No a? Use s = ¬Ω(u+v)t
üìù Example: Braking Car
A car at 25 m/s brakes and stops in 5 seconds. Find acceleration and stopping distance.

Given: u = 25 m/s, v = 0, t = 5 s

Acceleration: v = u + at ‚Üí 0 = 25 + a(5) ‚Üí a = -5 m/s¬≤

Distance: s = ¬Ω(u+v)t = ¬Ω(25+0)(5) = 62.5 m
Free Fall

Near Earth's surface, all objects fall with acceleration g = 9.8 m/s¬≤ (often use 10 m/s¬≤).

For upward motion, take up as positive: a = -g = -9.8 m/s¬≤

1.3 Projectile Motion

Key principle: Horizontal and vertical motions are independent.

  • Horizontal: constant velocity (no acceleration)
  • Vertical: constant acceleration g downward
  • Time is the same for both components
$$\text{Horizontal: } x = v_x t \text{ where } v_x = v_0\cos\theta$$ $$\text{Vertical: } y = v_y t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2 \text{ where } v_y = v_0\sin\theta$$ $$\text{Time of flight: } T = \frac{2v_0\sin\theta}{g}$$ $$\text{Max height: } H = \frac{(v_0\sin\theta)^2}{2g}$$ $$\text{Range: } R = \frac{v_0^2\sin(2\theta)}{g}$$
üí° Projectile Insights
  • Maximum range at 45¬∞ launch angle
  • Complementary angles give same range (30¬∞ and 60¬∞)
  • Trajectory is always a parabola
üìù Example
Ball kicked at 20 m/s at 30¬∞ angle. Find max height and range. (g = 10 m/s¬≤)

Components:
v‚Çì = 20cos(30¬∞) = 17.3 m/s
v·µß = 20sin(30¬∞) = 10 m/s

Max height: H = (10)¬≤/(2√ó10) = 5 m
Time: T = 2(10)/10 = 2 s
Range: R = 17.3 √ó 2 = 34.6 m

2.1 Newton's Laws

First Law (Inertia)

An object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a net force.

Inertia: tendency to resist changes in motion. Mass measures inertia.

Second Law
$$\vec{F}_{net} = m\vec{a}$$

Net force equals mass times acceleration. Acceleration is in direction of net force.

Third Law (Action-Reaction)

For every action, there's an equal and opposite reaction.

üí° Action-Reaction Pairs
  • Forces act on DIFFERENT objects
  • Equal magnitude, opposite direction
  • Same type of force
Common Forces
$$\text{Weight: } W = mg$$ $$\text{Friction: } f = \mu N$$ $$\text{Normal force: perpendicular to surface}$$
üìù Example
10 kg box pushed with 50 N force. Friction = 20 N. Find acceleration.

Net force: F_net = 50 - 20 = 30 N
Acceleration: a = F/m = 30/10 = 3 m/s¬≤

2.2 Momentum and Impulse

$$\text{Momentum: } \vec{p} = m\vec{v}$$ $$\text{Impulse: } \vec{J} = \vec{F}\Delta t = \Delta\vec{p}$$
üí° Conservation of Momentum
In a closed system (no external forces), total momentum is conserved: $$m_1v_{1i} + m_2v_{2i} = m_1v_{1f} + m_2v_{2f}$$
Types of Collisions

Elastic: Both momentum and kinetic energy conserved

Inelastic: Only momentum conserved (some KE lost to heat/sound)

Perfectly inelastic: Objects stick together

üìù Example: Collision
1000 kg car at 20 m/s hits stationary 1500 kg car. They stick. Find final velocity.

Before: p = 1000(20) + 1500(0) = 20,000 kg¬∑m/s
After: 20,000 = (1000+1500)v
Result: v = 8 m/s
